the timeline of how they cooked it.

tap any card to open the receipt. every fact links to a real source — screenshot it, send it to the group chat, whatever. the govt kept the paper less secure than your ex kept your secrets.

  1. 3 MAY 20262.27 million kids sit the exam+

    NEET-UG 2026 is conducted for 2.27 million aspirants — the single exam that decides who gets to be a doctor. no drama yet. that lasts about nine days.

    🧾 source: NEET 2026 controversy →
  2. 12 MAY 2026cancelled — a "guess paper" matched ~140 questions+

    NTA cancels the exam. a "guess paper" that circulated on WhatsApp and coaching centres in Sikar, Rajasthan overlapped with up to ~140 real questions (esp. Chemistry & Biology). the CBI takes over the probe. imagine studying for 2 years to lose to a WhatsApp forward.

    🧾 source: NEET 2026 controversy →
  3. 15 MAY 2026"just resit it lol" — re-exam set for 21 June+

    a re-test is scheduled for 21 June. lakhs of exhausted teenagers are told to peak, again, on command — no accountability offered, just a new date. the investigation later finds the same racket had also compromised the 2025 paper.

    🧾 source: NEET 2026 controversy →
  4. 6 JUN 2026the roaches assemble 🪳+

    the satirical Cockroach Janta Party (CJP) launches protests at Jantar Mantar. the name reclaims a Chief Justice's remark comparing unemployed youth to "cockroaches" and "parasites." Gen-Z looked at an insult and made it a brand. iconic behaviour honestly.

    🧾 source: Jantar Mantar protests →
  5. 16–22 JUN 2026Telegram banned for a week+

    instead of just, y'know, securing the paper, the Ministry blocks Telegram (16–22 June) to stop leaks; the Delhi High Court upholds it despite Telegram's challenge. bro banned an app to hide the homework he lost. the retest papers get flown around on Air Force aircraft.

    🧾 source: NEET 2026 controversy →
  6. 28 JUN 2026Sonam Wangchuk starts a hunger strike+

    Ladakhi environmentalist Sonam Wangchuk joins the agitation and begins an indefinite hunger strike, demanding accountability and Education Minister Dharmendra Pradhan's resignation. by 17 July he's lost 9.1 kg.

    🧾 source: Jantar Mantar protests →
  7. 18 JUL 2026police drag the hunger-striker to hospital+

    after 21 days, Delhi Police forcibly remove Wangchuk and admit him to Safdarjung Hospital despite his resistance. the optics: the state physically carrying off a starving man rather than answering him. cool. normal. very human.

    🧾 source: Jantar Mantar protests →
  8. 20 JUL 2026"Sansad Chalo" → tear gas & lathi charge+

    10,000+ students march toward Parliament. the response: lathi charges, tear gas, reportedly electrified barricades, ~60 protesters injured, internet shut off near Parliament, metro stations closed, and opposition leaders (incl. Rahul Gandhi) detained. the answer to "why did the exam leak?" was apparently "batons."

    🧾 source: IBTimes — police crackdown →
  9. 21 JUL 2026still. no. resignation.+

    opposition leaders detained; protesters reclaim the site. the Education Minister — who admitted "a breach in the command chain" — keeps his chair. the one demand at the centre of the whole movement is simply… ignored. meanwhile ≥12 students had already died by suicide in the fallout.

    🧾 source: Deccan Herald →
  10. 22 JUL 2026metro shut, CRPF flown in, court says "don't waste our time"+

    the DMRC shuts 16 central Delhi metro stations; 20 CRPF companies are airlifted from Kolkata and deployed. Rahul Gandhi is released after being detained outside the PM's residence; opposition leaders protest outside Parliament in solidarity. the CJI rejects an urgent plea over police brutality — "don't waste our time," refusing to review the footage. Wangchuk offers to end his strike if the govt promises no punitive action against students. protesters hold the site through heavy overnight rain.

    🧾 source: Jantar Mantar protests →
  11. 23 JUL 2026 · DAY 25Wangchuk drops a reel — the strike hits day 25+

    day 25 of the hunger strike. the 16 metro stations stay shut and Parliament stays gridlocked (see the Parliament Watch below). from the protest site, Sonam Wangchuk posts a reel — it's embedded in the "day 25" section further down. the tally so far: 0 resignations, 0 resolutions, 1 hunger strike entering week 4.

    🎬 watch the reel on Instagram →
  12. 23 JUL 2026Modi breaks silence — with fast-track courts, not a resignation+

    after weeks of saying nothing, the PM finally posts on X: "Nothing is more important than the welfare and future of our youth! We have decided to set up fast-track courts to ensure swift and stringent punishment for those involved in paper leaks." he adds that he's directed officials to act, and that anyone harming students' futures "will not be spared." Health Minister J.P. Nadda follows up by asking everyone not to "politicise" the issue.

    what the post somehow forgets to mention: the minister still sitting in his chair, the students who got tear-gassed, the FIRs filed against protesters, and the ₹1 crore compensation the families are asking for. fast-track courts for the leakers, zero-track accountability for the leadership. a whole tweet — and it answered none of the three demands. 🐍

    🧾 source: Organiser →
  13. 23–24 JUL 2026a midnight selfie video — and a secretary thrown overboard+

    late on the 23rd the PM posts a selfie video on the paper-leak row — his first direct video intervention since the agitation began. in it: the guilty "have been caught. they are now in jail," 22 lakh students got a retest, and a strong anti-paper-leak bill goes to the Cabinet on Friday and Parliament next week. no press conference, no questions taken, no statement to the House, which sat adjourned over this exact issue for the fourth consecutive day. Rahul Gandhi's review: a "pathetic midnight video."

    within a day the Appointments Committee of the Cabinet moves Higher Education Secretary Vineet Joshi out to the Department of Panchayati Raj, with Naresh Pal Gangwar taking over. an unelected bureaucrat absorbs the blame; Dharmendra Pradhan — the one name students have chanted for seven weeks — keeps his chair. the demand was a resignation. the delivery was a transfer order and a vertical video. 🤳

    🎬 the selfie video → 🧾 source: ANI → 🧾 source: Siasat →
  14. 24 JUL 2026 · LATESThe breaks the fast. nobody breaks the chair.+

    past midnight at Medanta, Gurugram, with Union ministers J.P. Nadda and Dr Jitendra Singh at the bedside, Sonam Wangchuk ends his hunger strike on day 26. he is 59. he has lost around 11 kg. in his own words: "just now… I finally broke my fast after 26 days." he ended it, he said on X, after "a long negotiation on various conditions and in view of possible violence in the country."

    what the state finally put on the table — no cases against people who protested peacefully at Jantar Mantar or marched on Parliament on 20 July; parliamentary discussion on the leaks and exam reform; "positive consideration" of compensation for the families of students who died; and that anti-leak bill. read that list again: the headline concession is a promise not to prosecute the victims. that's not a win, that's a hostage release. 🪳

    what's still missing: Dharmendra Pradhan. seven weeks of chanting one name and the man never stood up. Wangchuk says he'll explain the full conditions in a separate video "very soon" — so a fast that was public for 26 days ended on terms nobody outside the room has seen. CJP's answer, from founder Abhijeet Dipke, was immediate: thanks for the "extraordinary courage and sacrifice," and "the peaceful protest at Jantar Mantar will continue until Dharmendra Pradhan resigns." 26 days, 1 transfer order, 0 resignations.

🤝 24 jul · the deal, decoded

26 days of not eating, for a promise not to arrest anyone

a fast ends when the demand is met. this one ended when the demand was outlasted. here's what was actually traded, in daylight, for anyone still calling it a victory.

🎯 three demands, one delivery

the movement wanted Pradhan's resignation, accountability for the lathi charge, and relief for the 2.27M whose exam was voided. what arrived: a bill, a bureaucrat's transfer, and an assurance that students who got beaten also won't be charged for it. the leadership answered the leak downstream and never once looked upstream at itself.

🧾 source →

🚔 the concession is the confession

the top line of the deal is "no cases against those who protested peacefully." sit with that. the state's biggest gift to a 59-year-old who stopped eating for 26 days was agreeing not to prosecute people for peacefully protesting — a thing the constitution already covers. when "we won't punish you for your rights" is a bargaining chip, the chip was never theirs to spend.

🕛 the midnight timing

the PM's video drops late night, the fast ends past midnight, the Cabinet takes the bill up the very next morning. weeks of silence, then the entire sequence compressed into eight hours after dark — the news cycle gets a resolution headline before the country wakes up, and nobody has to answer a question in daylight. Rahul Gandhi's three-word review of the format: "pathetic midnight video."

🧾 source →

🤐 terms nobody has seen

a 26-day public fast ended on conditions Wangchuk says he'll explain in a separate video, "very soon." the whole point of a hunger strike is that it happens in public — the leverage is the visibility. ending it on undisclosed terms hands the other side the one thing it wanted all along: the story ends, the file stays closed.

🪳 the sit-in didn't end

within hours, CJP founder Abhijeet Dipke: the Jantar Mantar protest "will continue until Dharmendra Pradhan resigns." spokesman Ashutosh Ranka called fast-track courts insufficient without systemic reform. the government bought the headline — "activist ends fast" — and got exactly zero of the movement.

🤖 what an AI would've done on day 1

a chatbot with no ego and no seat to protect would've said it in one message on 28 June: here's the audit, here's the compensation, here's my resignation, here's the reform bill. 26 days, 11 kg and a hospital bed later, the state produced a fraction of that — and only once it had a midnight window to bury it in. less humanity than a chatbot isn't a joke line. it's the timeline.

🏛️ parliament watch · monsoon session 2026

what parliament "decided" (spoiler: nothing)

both houses agree there should be a debate. they cannot agree on how to have the debate. that's the entire update. 2.27M futures on hold over a procedure fight.

🟩 Lok Sabha lower house

opened in uproar over the leak, the police action and the resignation demand. Rahul Gandhi wrote to PM Modi seeking a debate and pushed for a discussion; the opposition walked out. President Droupadi Murmu's line to the House: "delay in exams is not fine — the guilty in recent paper leaks will be investigated and punished." (announcements > accountability, apparently.)

🧾 source →

🟦 Rajya Sabha upper house

adjourned amid the standoff. Parliamentary Affairs Minister Kiren Rijiju said the govt is "ready to discuss" — just not on the opposition's terms. Mallikarjun Kharge demanded a debate under Rule 267 and made it conditional on Pradhan's resignation. net result: repeated adjournments and a whole lot of nothing.

🧾 source →

📋 the verdict, as of 24 Jul: both Houses disrupted for the fourth consecutive day — no adjournment motion admitted, no resolution passed, no resignation, no re-exam relief announced in the House. just a deadlock over procedure. the PM's long-awaited response arrived on X, then as a midnight selfie video, never as a statement to Parliament. an anti-paper-leak bill — fast-track courts, stiffer sentences — goes to the Cabinet on the 24th and is promised in the House next week; until it's tabled, voted and passed, it is a press release with a deadline. a chatbot could've scheduled the meeting.

🤳 23–24 jul · the selfie video, decoded

why a selfie video, of all things?

a head of government answering a national scandal with a phone at arm's length is genuinely unusual. it isn't laziness — it's a format picked on purpose. here's the play, and why it's cheap.

▶ watch on Instagram →

🤳 the format is the message

a selfie video is the grammar of a friend, not a head of government. it manufactures intimacy while quietly deleting every mechanism of accountability: no press pack, no follow-up questions, no Parliament. he addressed the country on Instagram while both Houses sat adjourned over this exact issue for a fourth straight day. the one room where somebody gets to ask "so will he resign?" is precisely the room that got skipped.

🪑 the sacrificial bureaucrat

within a day: Higher Education Secretary Vineet Joshi shipped off to Panchayati Raj, Naresh Pal Gangwar in. an unelected civil servant takes the fall so the elected minister doesn't have to. it photographs like consequences and costs the leadership nothing — accountability theatre with a cast of one.

🧾 source →

📱 fighting the meme war on meme turf

this movement is Gen Z, Instagram-first and meme-native — Al Jazeera reported the kids had "flipped Modi's social media game," one protester noting "the memes are uniting us" and that criticising the PM is now "not only acceptable, but even cool." so the reply arrives as a reel. it meets the students on their platform precisely so it never has to meet them on their demand.

🧾 source →

📢 announcement > accountability

same trick as the X post a day earlier: fast-track courts to punish the leakers downstream, nothing that touches the leadership upstream. you can announce a court in an afternoon; you cannot announce your way out of a resignation. four leaks, 2.27M exams voided, ≥12 students gone — answered with a transfer order and a vertical video.
